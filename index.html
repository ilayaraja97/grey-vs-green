Bell Farms & Resort - A journey towards sustainability goals.

Dr. Sherimon P.C. Chairman, Bell Foods Group

Balancing Hospitality & Biodiversity

Companies should learn that protection of the environment should be its priority to reach long term goals and the best way to do that is to work with it.

The resort is built within a mangrove forest on Munroe Island. How do you balance maintaining biodiversity?

At Bell Farms & Resorts, sustainability is woven into our DNA. In Munroe Island's delicate mangrove ecosystem, we tread lightly—minimizing harm, restoring habitats, and innovating with eco-friendly designs like elevated walkways and stringent waste management. By collaborating with experts and locals, we're nurturing a thriving ecosystem that sets a new benchmark for responsible tourism.

Do you incorporate traditional farming techniques to keep the forest healthy?

We blend traditional wisdom with modern techniques. This includes organic shrimp farming that mimics natural habitats, mangrove-friendly farming that enhances growth, and community-led conservation to project and restore forests.

What species have returned to the area since farming operations began?

We have observed an encouraging return of native wildlife. Bird species such as Kingfishers, Egrets, and Cormorants are now frequently spotted. Water snakes have returned, and native fish varieties like Pearl Spot and prawns have shown an increased presence, highlighting how eco-friendly farming restores ecosystems.

Reality of AI and its Effects on the Environment

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has transformed everything in this world. It is not just a bubble in which investors just invest till they find profit, no! People are seeing irreversible changes— marvelous and horrifying. AI technology is a package; it is used for cancer and disease research while also being used for fraud, blackmailing and privacy breaches. It also has another huge impact which everyone tends to conveniently shove under the rug: the various environmental impacts of AI.

"A typical data center uses 300,000 gallons of water each day (equivalent to the demands of about 1,000 households), but large data centers can use an estimated 5 million gallons of water each day, equivalent to the needs of a town of up to 50,000 residents" according to brookings.edu. The water is actually used to cool down the data centers. We all know how important fresh water is and many countries and places don't even have fresh water so it is highly unethical to use fresh water for data centers.

The energy demands of AI data centers approach 1,050 TWh by 2026. A single ChatGPT request requires ten times more electricity than a Google search. Use AI wisely, avoid unnecessary prompts, and rely on standard efficiency where possible.

Why the Bee Population is Indispensable?

Farming is an age-old process that started about 12,000 years ago during the Neolithic Revolution. The beauty of farming is that it helped humans make food banks so that they can survive winters. But how is the bee population connected?

Experts say that one out of every three bites of food we eat is thanks to pollinators like bees. They are responsible for the fruits and vegetables that both animals and humans depend on. Without them, we would lose favorite foods like apples, almonds, berries, and even coffee.

Despite their importance, the bee population is steadily declining. In some parts of the world, beekeepers have seen their colonies drop by 30% to 40% each year due to poaching, sheer deforestation, and toxic modern pesticides that confuse their internal navigation. If bees disappear, the cost of food will skyrocket and famines could follow. We need to protect their natural habitats and reduce the use of harmful sprays.

Ethanol, an Evil in Disguise for Thirsty India

India is highly dependent on oil imports. To solve this, the government is aggressively pushing ethanol blending, up to E20 and planning for E85. Ethanol in India is primarily derived from water-intensive crops like sugarcane.

At first glance, ethanol seems like a silver bullet. However, the problem stems from the fact that sugarcane demands thousands of billions of liters of water. We are already failing to provide potable drinking water to 600 million citizens facing extreme water stress. Prioritizing fuel crops over human survival feels like a repeat of colonial indigo farming.

Moreover, we are over-fertilizing our farmlands, draining subsidy funds (₹2.04 lakh crore expected), and destroying soil quality. The solution? Investing in ethanol is dangerous for food security. Focusing on EVs, public transport, solar energy, and reducing unnecessary vehicles is the true path forward.

Are Tall Skyscrapers the Answer to Sustainability?

The human population is over 8 billion, causing a massive housing shortage. Low-density single-family homes take up enormous land space and make sprawling infrastructure unaffordable and environmentally harmful.

Flats are highly efficient. They save space, centralize demand, and reduce urban sprawl. But what about skyscrapers (buildings over 150m tall)? Skyscrapers are a different story. They contain massive amounts of empty "spire" space just for vanity height, and their maintenance—like pumping water to top floors, massive HVAC systems, trucks hauling waste—makes them wildly unsustainable energy sinks.

Instead of building glorified lightning rods that exploit cheap labor, the government should focus on creating highly efficient green flats incorporating vertical forests, making cities genuinely breathable, affordable, and sustainable.

The Global Death Race for Rare Minerals

The push for a green transition—EVs, solar panels, and wind turbines—has traded our dependence on oil for a new reliance on rare earth minerals like Nickel, Cobalt, Lithium, and Silver. A single EV battery requires 8kg of lithium, 35kg of nickel, and 14kg of cobalt.

These materials are often mined under horrific conditions, including child labor in places like the Democratic Republic of Congo. Moreover, the environmental cost is staggering: mining just one ton of rare earth metals produces about 2,000 tons of toxic waste. Deep-sea mining threatens to wipe out undiscovered species.

Currently, less than 5% of lithium-ion batteries are recycled. To achieve real sustainability, we must mandate comprehensive recycling policies and fund research into alternative battery tech—like sodium-ion batteries—before we destroy the planet to save it.

Quarrying Should Stop But How?

Quarrying strips mountains of M-Sand, granite, and limestone, turning green peaks into empty, broken land. Taking out the foundations of these mountains removes vital support, leaving the remaining geography fundamentally unstable.

Mountains do more than look pretty; they control the weather, trap rain clouds to feed groundwater networks, and cool the surrounding environment with their thick forests. Ignoring these facts has deadly consequences. Destabilized slopes and deforested hillsides have severely increased fatal landslides and devastating floods in regions like the Western Ghats.

To survive the future, we must shift away from destructive extraction. We need to champion Green Building methods, aggressively recycle demolition waste, and implement strict environmental protections before we permanently lose the natural structures that sustain our water and air.
